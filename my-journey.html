My Journey

Back in 2019, fresh out of college, I had no plans set in stone. I started backpacking across Himachal, Uttarakhand, Goa, and Karnataka, taking up volunteering gigs in homestays, hostels, and eco-retreats. Then came my first real encounter with farming—at Varanashi Organic Farms in Karnataka.

The days were raw—sweating under the sun, working alongside farmers, eating fresh organic food straight from the land. By the time my volunteering ended, I felt a change—physically, mentally, and emotionally. Looking back, I didn’t realize then how deeply that experience would shape my future.

Then COVID happened, and everything paused. My travel stopped. I found myself back home, involved in the family business—something that never quite felt like me. I attempted CFA as a way out, but when that didn’t work, I spiraled into uncertainty. That’s when I turned towards wellness, not as a business but as a way to heal myself.

Wellness became an obsession. The perfectionist in me wanted the **best** herbs, the **purest** ingredients. My curiosity led me back to farmers, sourcing directly from the land, just like I had done at Varanashi. That’s when something clicked.

From there, I started experimenting—sourcing organic **Amla & Moringa**, processing them at home, branding them into high-quality wellness products. But the market was flooded with similar things. I shifted towards **natural cosmetics** and **Ayurvedic oils**—developing formulations for face masques, herbal cleansers, hair oils. But then, reality hit. Manufacturing cosmetics required extensive licenses, industrial setups, and a team of Ayurvedic doctors—something I wasn’t ready to compromise my freedom for.

Then came Almond Oil. I was already making it as an ingredient for my oils. I had trusted sourcing in Kashmir, and my processing setup was in place. It made sense to dedicate myself fully to this. I moved my setup to my home’s drawing room to ensure **absolute quality control and a homemade essence.** Now, this is what I do—every single step, from sourcing to crafting, is something I enjoy deeply.

Kasar Devi Almond Oil isn’t just a business—it’s a process, a journey, and a pursuit of purity.
